What is Tenun Bengkala?

Tenun (Woven fabric) is traditional fabric made by manually weaving threads using a traditional loom. This process involves arranging the warp (vertical) and weft (horizontal) threads to create a particular pattern or motif. Woven fabrics often reflect a region's cultural identity through their motifs, colors and techniques.

In the context of Tenun Bengkala (Bengkala Village Weaving), this cloth not only functions as a textile product, but also as a symbol of culture and local wisdom of the Balinese people. The distinctive motifs contain philosophical meaning, while the manufacturing process involves high skills that have been passed down from generation to generation. For the deaf and mute community of Bengkala Village, making woven cloth is also a means of expressing their creativity and strengthening community inclusivity..

Making Process Of Tenun Bengkala

Material Preparation

Yarn of cotton, silk, or other natural fibers spun and dyed according to the desired pattern.
Natural dyes, such as from plants or chemicals, are used to color yarn.

Making Patterns

The pattern or motif is designed first. For ikat weaving, threads are tied in certain parts before dyeing to form the desired motif.

Arrangement of Threads (Melungsi)

The warp (vertical) threads are arranged on the loom, ensuring proper tension to produce quality fabric.

 Weaving Process

Using a traditional loom (ATBM or gedogan), the weft (horizontal) threads are woven through the warp threads. Weavers move tools with their hands and feet in sync to produce patterns and textures of fabric.

This process can take around 1 week and a maximum of 1 month depending on the complexity of the motif
